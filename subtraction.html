Method 01 (Fastest)
Left-to-Right Subtraction

"Never subtract from right-to-left. By stripping away large chunks first (Hundreds or Tens), you quickly reduce the size of the problem in your head, avoiding painful carryovers!"

2-Digit Problem: 83 - 27
83 - 27
1. Subtract the Tens (83 - 20)↓
63
2. Subtract the Units (63 - 7)↓
56
63 - 7 is easier if you think 63 - 3 (which is 60) and then - 4 more.
56

362

Method 02
Same Difference Rule

"If you add the exact same number to both the top and bottom values of a subtraction problem, the distance (difference) between them NEVER changes! We use this to turn messy bottom numbers into clean zeros."

2-Digit Problem: 73 - 38
38 is very annoying. Let's add 2 everywhere to make it 40.↓
73 (+2) = 75
-
38 (+2) = 40
Now it's just 75 - 40!↓
38 is hard to subtract. 40 is easy. Adding 2 to BOTH numbers keeps the gap identical!
35
3-Digit Problem: 512 - 287
287 is annoying. Let's add 13 to both sides to make it 300!↓
512 (+13) = 525
-
287 (+13) = 300
Just do 525 - 300!↓
225
Mixed Problem: 415 - 96
96 is annoying. Add 4 to both sides!↓
415 (+4) = 419
-
96 (+4) = 100
Just do 419 - 100!↓

Method 03
Rounding & Adjusting

"If you are subtracting a number ending in 8 or 9, go ahead and subtract the entire next ten or hundred because it's easier. Then, just 'pay back' the small amount you over-subtracted by adding it."

2-Digit Problem: 81 - 39
Pretend we are subtracting 40 instead of 39.↓
81 - 40 = 41
We subtracted 1 too much. Add it back!↓
41 + 1
42
3-Digit Problem: 654 - 299

We subtracted 1 too much. Add it back.↓
354 + 1

Mixed Problem: 342 - 98
Pretend we are subtracting exactly 100!↓
342 - 100 = 242
We subtracted 2 too much. Add them back.↓
242 + 2

Method 04
The Cashier's Method (Counting Up)

"Have you seen cashiers count change? They don't subtract; they ADD UP from the purchase price until they hit your bill! Subtraction is just the distance between two numbers. Walk forward!"

2-Digit Problem: 72 - 48
Start at 48. Let's walk forward to 72.↓
48 ➔ 50 (+2)
50 ➔ 70 (+20)
70 ➔ 72 (+2)
Add your steps: 2 + 20 + 2↓
Think of this as traveling from 48 to 72. You take small jumps to reach easy landmarks!
24

800 ➔ 814 (+14)

419

Method 05 (Bonus Magic)
Base Zero (Vedic Trick)

"Subtracting from 100 or 1000 usually requires painful chain borrowing. Vedic mathematics teaches a secret rule for this exact scenario: Subtract ALL digits from 9, except the very last digit from 10!"

2-Digit Problem: 100 - 43
All from 9, last from 10!↓
9 - 4 = 5
10 - 3 = 7
Just push the answers together!↓
Because we subtracted 3 from 10 instead of 9, we effectively "borrowed" from the hundreds column already. Splitting the digits this way works for any multiple of 10!
57
3-Digit Problem: 1000 - 347
First two from 9, last from 10!↓
9 - 3 = 6
9 - 4 = 5
10 - 7 = 3
653
Mixed Problem: 500 - 64
Split it into parts! (400) + (100 - 64)↓
Keep 400 safe
9 - 6 = 3
10 - 4 = 6
Put it back together! 400 and 36.↓
436
