Quick Recall (1-50)
Method 01 (The magic 25)
Near 50 Base Method

"When squaring numbers near 50, use 25 as your anchor. See how far the number is from 50 (the deviation). Add/subtract that deviation to 25 for the first half, then square the deviation for the second half!"

Problem: 53² (53 × 53)
53
1. Distance from 50
+3
53 is 3 more than 50. Our deviation is 3.
2. First Half (Anchor 25)
25 + 3 = 28
Writes: 28 _ _
Always use 25 for numbers near 50. Add our deviation (3) to it.
3. Second Half (Square it)
3² = 09
Writes: 28 09
Square the deviation. Use two digits! (3² is 09, not just 9)
↓
2809
Method 02
Near 100 Base Method

"For numbers near 100, the number itself is the anchor! Subtract/Add the distance from 100 to the original number for the first half, then square the distance for the second half."

Problem: 96²
96
1. Distance from 100
-4
96 is 4 less than 100. Our deviation is -4.
2. First Half (Self-Anchor)
96 - 4 = 92
Writes: 92 _ _
Take the original number (96) and apply the deviation (-4) to it.
3. Second Half (Square it)
4² = 16
Writes: 92 16
Square the deviation (4). That fills the final two spots.
↓
9216
Method 03
The "Five-End" Trick

"This is the fastest of them all. If it ends in 5, the answer ALWAYS ends in 25. For the first half, just multiply the tens digit by the next highest digit. Instant answer!"

Problem: 75²
75
1. Tens × (Tens + 1)
7 × 8 = 56
Writes: 56 _ _
Take the first digit (7) and multiply it by 8.
75
2. Always 25
5² = 25
Writes: 56 25
The square of any number ending in 5 always finishes with 25.
↓
5625
Method 04
Universal 2-Digit Square

"No fancy base near you? Use this universal algebraic shortcut: Square the first, Multiply first x second x 2, then Square the third. Push them together!"

Problem: 32²
32
1. Square the First (A²)
3² = 9
The first part involves squaring the tens digit.
32
2. Double Product (2AB)
2 × (3×2) = 12
Multiply both digits together (6), then double it (12). Carry the 1!
32
3. Square the Last (B²)
2² = 4
Square the unit digit. (Result: 9 | 12 | 4)
↓ Carry the 1 from '12' to the '9'
1024
Method 05
The "Step from Zero" Trick

"Numbers ending in 1 are just one step away from a much easier square. Square the round number, then add the sum of the numbers to it. It sounds complex but it's pure addition!"

Problem: 31²
1. Square the Base
30² = 900
Square the round number just below it (30 x 30).
2. Add the Sum
30 + 31 = 61
Add the base (30) and the target (31) together.
3. Final Addition
900 + 61
Combine your two easy numbers!
↓
961
